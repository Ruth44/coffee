Ethiopia – the land of coffee!

Ethiopia was the birthplace of coffee in 850 AD, according to history. After eating coffee beans, Kaldi, a young goat herder, observed an improvement in the energy and liveliness of his herd. The world's obsession with coffee began when Kaldi tried the beans and observed the results. Yirgacheffe, a small region in southern Ethiopia, is located within Sidama and is known for its beans. Because of the dense vegetation, fertile soil, and high elevations, the plants grow naturally. Ethiopia has a tropical climate with mild wet and dry seasons.

Yirgacheffe plants can be cultivated without the use of agricultural chemicals due to the perfect climate. From October to December, Yirgacheffe beans are harvested. Yirgacheffe is an excellent choice if you're looking for a green, organic coffee.

Coffee is Ethiopia’s biggest export. In 2008, the Ethiopia commodities market officially began trading coffee. Today, Ethiopia is the world’s fifth largest coffee-producing nation and grows the most coffee in Africa. It produces 846,575,000 pounds of coffee per year. About 15 million Ethiopian citizens work in coffee production.
